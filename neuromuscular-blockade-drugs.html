Drugs used for neuromuscular blockade during general anesthesia

March 04, 2016 · Table ·

  • pharmacology
  • anesthesia

There are two classes of neuromuscular blocking drugs (NMBD) used as paralytic agents during the induction of general anesthesia–depolarizing and nondepolarizing.

Some reasons for using neuromuscular blockade in the OR:

  • Paralyze vocal cords and jaw muscules to allow for endotracheal intubation
  • Paralyze respiratory muscles so that ventilation can be controlled
  • Paralyze the abdominal muscles intraoperatively during abdominal or laparoscopic surgery

NMBDs do not have analgesic or anesthetic effects and should always be given after the patient is sufficiency anesthetized

Due to risk for acute hyperkalemia, caution when giving succinylcholine to burn or trauma patients, and to children due to possible unrecognized muscular dystrophy.

Sugammadex is a newer drug that can be used for reversal of nondepolarizing NMBDs such as rocuronium. It basically encapsulates and inactivates the NMBD (very different from neostigmine) and has no effect at the neuromuscular junction3.
